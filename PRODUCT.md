# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Ubroker serves Brazilian real estate brokers who need to run a high-value commercial operation without depending entirely on a traditional agency. The primary user is the broker working leads, properties, partnerships, activities, referrals, inbox messages, and AI-assisted follow-up from a single authenticated workspace. A secondary internal/admin user monitors the broader Ubroker operation: users, support, partnerships, leads, inbox, properties, AI, finance, configuration, and audit trails.

## Product Purpose

Ubroker gives brokers one operational home for leads, pipeline, property inventory, partnerships, recurring referral monetization, AI assistance, omnichannel inbox, and business performance. Success means a broker can see what needs attention, move opportunities forward, collaborate with other brokers, and understand revenue impact without stitching together several tools.

The public landing page introduces the promise, but the default design context is the product surface: dashboard, app shell, admin panels, forms, tables, cards, drawers, and workflow states.

## Positioning

Ubroker is the control center for independent high-standard brokers: the network, CRM, AI assistant, and monetization layer in one product.

## Brand Personality

Confident, precise, and commercially sharp. The product should feel like a serious operating system for brokers, not a generic CRM skin: calm enough for repeated daily work, but opinionated enough to make the broker feel in command of a premium business.

## Anti-references

Do not drift into generic SaaS dashboards with decorative gradients, oversized marketing cards, or vague AI sparkle language. Avoid real-estate cliché visuals that feel like a property portal rather than a broker operating system. Avoid heavy visual decoration in authenticated surfaces; density, clarity, and trust matter more than spectacle.

## Design Principles

Lead with the next useful action. Every app surface should make the broker's next move obvious: contact, qualify, schedule, propose, partner, invite, collect, or investigate.

Make money and momentum legible. VGV, commissions, SaaS monetization, lead flow, partnership status, and risk signals should be scannable without turning the UI into a wall of metrics.

Preserve a premium operating tone. Use restraint, hierarchy, and precise language so the product feels like a serious workspace for high-value transactions.

Separate broker work from admin governance. Broker screens should optimize daily flow; admin screens should optimize oversight, exception handling, finance, risk, and auditability.

Use AI as operational leverage, not decoration. AI should appear where it qualifies, summarizes, recommends, detects risk, or saves time.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Default to accessible product UI conventions: keyboard-reachable controls, visible focus states, readable contrast, clear labels, non-color-only status indicators, and reduced-motion-friendly interactions. The current codebase is web-first and should remain usable across desktop and mobile browser viewports.
